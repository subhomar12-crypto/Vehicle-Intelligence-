# The $210 Billion Problem

## A Global Crisis in Vehicle Maintenance

**The Worldwide Impact:**

| Region | Annual Cost of Unplanned Vehicle Repairs |
|--------|------------------------------------------|
| **North America** | $73 Billion |
| **Europe** | $58 Billion |
| **Asia Pacific** | $52 Billion |
| **Middle East & Africa** | $18 Billion |
| **Latin America** | $9 Billion |
| **TOTAL WORLDWIDE** | **$210 Billion** |

*Source: Global automotive aftermarket analysis, preventable maintenance failures*

## The Human Cost

**For Individual Drivers:**
- **$700+** average annual cost of unexpected car repairs
- **1.2 breakdowns** per person per year
- **4.5 hours** wasted stranded waiting for help
- **30% higher costs** for emergency vs. scheduled repairs

**For Parents of Teen Drivers:**
- **11 teenagers die daily** in car crashes (CDC)
- **56% of parents** constantly worry about their teen's driving
- No real-time visibility into driving behavior

**For Fleet Operators (The Biggest Pain Point):**
- **$15,000** average cost per vehicle downtime incident
- **20% of fuel budget** lost to theft and inefficiency
- **37% of fleet breakdowns** are preventable with predictive maintenance
- **Zero visibility** into which vehicle will fail next

## The Core Problem

Modern vehicles generate **25GB of data per hour** through their OBD-II port. This data contains early warning signs of failures, efficiency problems, and safety issues.

**But this data is:**
- Invisible to vehicle owners
- Only readable by expensive dealership tools ($500+ per diagnostic)
- Never analyzed for patterns or predictions
- Completely ignored until something breaks

**The Opportunity:**
If we could predict just **10%** of these failures before they happen, we'd save **$21 billion annually** and prevent thousands of accidents.
